# L03: Creating Cryptolaw for the U.C.C. (Reyes 2021) x 0xagentprivacy

Provenance: SSRN blocked (Cloudflare challenge) and the lexon.org/reyes.html fallback 404'd; text obtained from a 2024-03-18 Wayback Machine snapshot of the W&L Scholarly Commons open-access PDF (78 Wash. & Lee L. Rev. 1521), extracted with pdftotext; the inline Lexon/Solidity figures are absent from the extraction, so code commentary is matched to Appendices A-D.

## What the letter claims (5 sentences maximum, no quotes over 14 words)

The U.C.C. Article 9 filing system is a notice system that routinely fails to deliver actual notice, exposing even sophisticated parties to catastrophic loss, illustrated by a $1.5B In re Motors Liquidation hypothetical. Reyes builds a working smart-contract U.C.C.-1 financing statement in Lexon that does not merely digitize the paper form but performs Article 9 provisions directly, so the filing system works as intended for the first time. The prototype stays compatible with existing law: it implements nine sections, reduces risk of loss under six more, leaves adjudicative provisions untouched, and needs only a 9-521 safe-harbor confirmation plus one of two filing-office adoption routes. Five optional innovations emerge, several endogenously from the act of codifying law: search by public key, hash-identified collateral, a reminder-fee escrow, digital-asset collateral escrow, and debtor electronic authorization against fraudulent filings. Because a non-programmer law professor wrote the code in plain English, Reyes argues Lexon democratizes computational law and grounds a wider duty of technological competence for lawyers.

## Convergence findings (one subsection per assigned axis: A5, A7)

### A5. Grammar to graph (keystone)

**Roles are bound nouns compiled to key pairs.** The definitions section (Part II.B.1, pp. 1553-54) declares Filer, Debtor, Secured Party, and Filing Office each as a person; the Solidity compile target turns each into an address payable, that is, a public-private key pair through which the party operates in the system. These are deterministic entity nodes: exactly what a Bonfire graph extracts probabilistically from conversation, here produced by grammar. Binding is per-contract-instance, and under U.C.C. 9-509 cmt. 2 the filer's identity is legally immaterial (p. 1556), so the role slot rather than the personhood carries the meaning. That is the structuralist move the A5 axis predicts, verified in statutory material.

**The pass-through principle confirms the keystone.** Reyes reports (Part II.A, pp. 1546-47, drawing on Diedrich) that Lexon carries no representation of thought beyond the language input itself: the compiler machinery processes the natural language as if it were the program, with no translation of meaning into a separate bit-level representation. Her Ma citations (notes 111 and 126, pp. 1546, 1548) add the context-free-grammar frame in which syntactic structure is both projection and vessel of function. Meaning rides in grammar and typed relations, not in nouns, and this arrives from a second, independent author.

**The type vocabulary is a graph schema in miniature.** Person maps to a blockchain address (note 154, p. 1554), data to a unique identifier such as a cryptographic hash (note 162, p. 1555), time to a one-second-precision timestamp (p. 1561), binary to yes/no (p. 1561), plus amount and text (Appendix A, pp. 1585-86). Clause predicates (fix, certify, pay, file, declare, send, return, terminate) are the typed edges, and every clause parses as actor-may-predicate-object. Appendix A is therefore a complete, lossless triple serialisation of a statutory instrument: simultaneously readable form, executable code, and exact subgraph.

**The financing statement is a content-minimal bilateral relationship record.** A U.C.C.-1 discloses debtor, secured party, and a collateral indication, never the underlying security agreement, and is searchable only through the debtor-name index (note 146, p. 1552; 9-519(c), p. 1567). The system's documented failure is too little signal reaching legitimate searchers: Reyes lists five failure factors (Part I.A, pp. 1535-36) and observes that the system regularly fails to reach consensus about shared facts (note 10, p. 1527). VRCs face the inverse failure: too much signal reaching illegitimate parties. Same disclosure dial, opposite failure modes. This symmetry holds against the full text and stands as a clean candidate finding for the synthesis.

**Hash-as-reference implements commitment-without-disclosure inside a public record.** The file number can itself be a hash of the statement data (p. 1557 and note 173), and collateral can be identified by a hash of its serial number so the reference is exact while the content stays outside the record (Part III.A.2, p. 1569). A public-legible subgraph with opaque leaf content: the reconstruction ceiling drawn as topology, derived independently inside commercial law.

**The corpus is already treated as a queryable graph.** Part III.A.1 (p. 1568) enables search by file number, debtor public-key address, and secured-party public-key address via middleware or The Graph, with no new indexing duty on the office; the Conclusion (pp. 1582-83) resolves the $1.5B hypothetical by cross-comparing searches over collateral, secured party, and file number, a multi-edge graph query in all but name. Lexon 0.3's List of Amendments (Appendix D, pp. 1607-08) links statements by Amended Statement Filing Number: cross-record typed edges, a promise graph spanning contract instances.

**Codifying law surfaces the implicit.** Diedrich's correspondence reproduced at note 163 (pp. 1555-56) describes the puzzling triviality of Lexon code: writing law as code forces explicit formalization of background facts everyone takes for granted, such as that a filer exists or that the office assigns the number and date. For the knowledge-graph downstream this means controlled grammar surfaces relational structure that probabilistic extraction would systematically miss, because humans never utter it.

### A7. Terms as executable law

**The compatibility boundary, exactly.** Part II.B.3 (p. 1562) triages every relevant provision into three registers. Implements: 9-502(a), 9-510, 9-511, 9-515, 9-516, 9-519, 9-520, 9-521, 9-522. Reduces risk of loss from: 9-503, 9-504, 9-506, 9-509, 9-517, 9-524. Does not interfere with: 9-512, 9-513, 9-518, 9-525, 9-526, 9-527. Filing mechanics execute; naming, description, and error doctrines stay prose but shrink in risk; adjudicative provisions such as 9-518 wrongful-record claims stay entirely outside the code. This triage is the critical datum the letter brief asked for: the executable/prose boundary MyTerms must walk, already walked once for secured transactions.

**The compatibility argument has a reusable four-step structure** (Part II.C, pp. 1564-66): mirror the fields of the statutorily accepted safe-harbor form (9-521) so the executable version inherits acceptance; execute procedural rules while leaving every legal definition constant (p. 1554); name the single amendment needed, a 9-521 confirmation that a smart-contract-based electronic form qualifies for the existing safe harbor; and offer two adoption routes, either re-designating the filing office under 9-501(a)(2), which need not be governmental, or legislating authorization for the existing office on the Wyoming H.B. 0142 model (note 206). The five innovations are strictly optional and severable, so declining them does not break the base statement (p. 1567).

**Law-as-program is literal, not metaphor.** In the lapse, continuation, and clearing clauses the Lexon code simultaneously restates the statute and performs it, acting as its own specification: "The law in the books literally becomes the law in action" (Part II.B.3, 78 Wash. & Lee L. Rev. at 1560). This is the strongest structural convergence with the contractual privacy paradigm: no gap between the stated term and the executed term, hence no room for consent theatre.

**Permission semantics are voluntary and role-scoped.** May denotes the single actor allowed to initiate a given clause; nobody else can (p. 1557, citing the Lexon Bible). Enforcement is incentive rather than compulsion: smart contracts cannot force a party to act, they incentivize performance (Part III.A.3, p. 1570), and the filing office's reminder duty is expressly optional and paid through a refundable escrowed fee. Obligations here behave as Promise Theory promises, not impositions, arriving unprompted in a statutory setting.

**Transparency is a design constraint for public computational law.** Because the filing system is public, the code must retain the readability of the one-page PDF form; dual readability is Reyes's answer to the computational-opacity literature (Part II.A, pp. 1548, 1570-71; Conclusion, p. 1583). For MyTerms this is the readable-by-judges requirement met at the artifact level, and the reminder-fee clause, 9 lines of Lexon, is her accessibility exhibit.

**The PIP List plus debtor e-authorization is proto-bilateral.** Flagged public-key addresses trigger a manual hold, and the debtor releases the filing by signing with their private key (Part III.A.5, pp. 1574-76). A unilateral filing becomes conditionally bilateral: the counterparty's cryptographic assent gates effectiveness. This is the closest the paper comes to VRC co-signing, though Reyes frames it as fraud control, not relationship formation.

**Executable need not mean contractual.** The financing statement is not itself a contract, and most smart contracts are tools for performing obligations, including statutory ones (Part I.C, pp. 1543-44; Part III.B, p. 1582). MyTerms terms can therefore be executable without first being forced through contract-formation doctrine.

## Divergences and false friends

1. **Goal-level divergence under mechanism-level convergence.** The entire filing system exists to publicize, to put the world on notice (p. 1534); the Relationship Privacy Paradigm exists to hold reconstruction below 1. The convergence lives only in the machinery (content-minimal records, hash commitments, key-scoped roles), never in the objective. Do not overclaim this one.
2. **False friend: role binding is not identity binding.** Lexon person bindings are per-instance, static, and in the JavaScript runtime explicitly trustful; the Appendix C instructions (p. 1593) note that anyone can manipulate contract state, with only the signed log protected. Demo keys are on-file RSA-2048 .key files with passphrases (p. 1605). No rotation, no personhood grounding, no KERI analogue, on instruments with lifetimes of 5 years plus continuations.
3. **False friend: escrow inside the record.** Part III.A.4 has the filing record itself custody digital-asset collateral. A VRC never custodies value; a relationship record that holds assets is a different object with different failure modes.
4. **Compile targets diverge on evidence retention.** The Solidity Terminate_and_Clear uses selfdestruct(debtor) (Appendix B, p. 1591), implementing record clearance as contract destruction, the opposite of an append-only evidence substrate; the JavaScript version instead flags the instrument terminated and keeps the signed log.
5. **Public law versus private ordering.** Reyes executes statute run by a public office; MyTerms is a person proffering private terms. Her argument leans on a statutory safe-harbor form (9-521) with no MyTerms analogue: the argument structure transfers, the anchor does not. IEEE P7012 would have to play the role of the accepted standard form.
6. **The designated intermediary survives, deliberately.** Reyes retreats from her 2017 position of eliminating the filing office and relies on its continued relevance for political feasibility (note 204, p. 1565). Bounded discretion by a designated neutral, an A6-flavored instinct, and a divergence from any fully disintermediated reading of agentprivacy.
7. **False friend: smart contract as a legal category.** State laws that tried to accommodate smart contracts in UETA effectively carved them out of coverage (p. 1582, note 268). Warning for agentprivacy: labeling VRC promise bundles contracts invites the same category error and the same legislative damage.
8. **Unilateral versus co-signed formation.** Every may clause has exactly one permitted initiator; nothing in the prototype is co-signed at formation. The VRC's bilateral bind has no counterpart here except the optional PIP countersignature path.

## Extractable mechanisms (what ports into agentprivacy V5/V6, concretely)

1. **Definitions-block as node emitter.** The TERMS section compiles to typed entity nodes (person keyed by public key, collateral keyed by hash), and clauses compile to typed permission edges of the form actor, may, verb, object. A direct worked spec for the Lexon-to-graph compiler target (HENNING_BRIEF probe iii) and for the bonfires deterministic write path; Appendices A and D are ready-made test fixtures.
2. **Hash-identified references.** File number as hash of statement data, collateral as hash of serial number: exact, collision-checked reference with zero content disclosure. Ports into V5/V6 as the pattern for VRC-adjacent public records, and is the natural seed for a public-subgraph/opaque-edge CTR-LEX candidate.
3. **Closed enumerations as controlled vocabulary.** Lexon 0.3's means-one-of construction, exhibited by the UCC Category of Collateral list with 15 values (Appendix D, p. 1607), makes controlled vocabularies first-class grammar, portable as node-label taxonomies in the graph overlay.
4. **The Reyes triage taxonomy** (implements, reduces risk of, does not interfere with) ports directly as the method for splitting a MyTerms term sheet into executable clauses, prose clauses, and adjudicative residue. It also rhymes with the V6 register discipline of proven, conjectured, speculative. Concrete use: classify each IEEE P7012 term into one of the three registers before drafting its Lexon clause.
5. **The four-step compatibility ladder** (mirror the accepted form, execute rules unchanged, name the one amendment, offer dual adoption routes) is the argument skeleton for making a MyTerms agreement court-readable under existing law; reuse it in HENNING_BRIEF probe (i).
6. **May-clause as per-role voluntary initiation right**, the drafting primitive for Swordsman boundary promises. Sketch, drafted by this chronicle and not present in the letter (conjecture in the design register): LEX MyTerms Agreement. "Person" is a person. "Counterparty" is a person. "Swordsman" is a person. The Person fixes the Counterparty, and fixes the Purpose. Clause: Accept Terms. The Counterparty may accept the Terms, and certify the Acceptance Time as the current time. Clause: Guard Sharing. The Swordsman may, if data leaves the stated Purpose, declare Breach and notify the Person. Three clauses, Payer/Payee style, roles as bound nouns.
7. **Reminder-fee escrow** (9 lines of Lexon, Part III.A.3): a paid, optional, refundable notification promise between two roles. Ports as the template for bounded service promises between First Person agents and counterparties: promise offered, priced, forgivable by refund.
8. **PIP List plus key-signature release.** Counterparty signature as a gate on the effectiveness of a unilateral act. Ports as the minimal upgrade path from unilateral Lexon promises toward bilateral VRC co-signing, by adding a may-authorize clause held by the affected party.
9. **In passing, A4 material for that chronicle's owner.** The JavaScript log() (Appendix C, p. 1605) signs each entry per actor (RSA-SHA256) and appends a rolling SHA-256 chain digest of the whole log, with bundle() and send() shipping code, state, and log by email. A working micro-blockchain evidence substrate private to the parties holding the bundle: candidate evidence for the epsilon(pi) substrate question.

## Open questions (numbered, maximum 5)

1. Does Lexon need a KERI-style rotation layer for long-lived instruments? The paper supplies evidence the gap is real: 5-year lapse plus continuations against static per-instance key bindings and file-based demo keys.
2. What plays the 9-521 safe-harbor role for MyTerms: can IEEE P7012 conformance function as the accepted form a court must not refuse, or is new legislation unavoidable?
3. Reyes keeps adjudicative provisions (9-518, 9-506) in prose; where does the equivalent line fall for a privacy agreement: breach declaration executable, with remedy and forgiveness left as prose? And who is the designated neutral in a MyTerms deployment, given her political argument for retaining the office?
4. Can Lexon's one-initiator may semantics express a co-signed bilateral bind at formation, where both must act for either edge to exist, or only the sequential offer-then-authorize pattern the PIP List exhibits?
5. How much implicit ceremony context can the puzzling-triviality effect force explicit before what remains is exactly the bilateral proverb? Conjecture: the opaque remainder is stable under increasing formalization, and that remainder is the floor that keeps R below 1.
