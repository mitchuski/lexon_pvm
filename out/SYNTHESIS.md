# SYNTHESIS: Lexon x 0xagentprivacy, the eight-axis verdict

Scope: this synthesis merges the five convergence chronicles (L01 Diedrich 2023, L02 Diedrich 2017, L03 Reyes 2021, L04 Clack 2021, L05 Idelberger 2020) per the P2 spec of ANALYSIS_PLAN.md. Verdicts follow the plan's three registers: structural convergence (same mechanism, different vocabulary), terminological convergence (same vocabulary, different mechanism, flagged as false friend), divergence (genuine disagreement worth preserving). Every verdict is grounded in chronicle content; genuine disagreements preserved inside a converging axis are recorded in the axis section and not smoothed. The V6 discipline applies throughout: proven, conjectured, and speculative are separate registers and nothing is upgraded.

## A1. Performative agreement: terminological convergence

**Convergences.** All three letters that touch the axis share one structural move with the RPP. L01 frames deployment on an unstoppable substrate as felicitous performative speech in Austin's sense, credits the framing in its footnote 7 to David Bovil (the letter's spelling), and adds that the parse is the meaning: the compiler's abstract syntax tree carries the document's meaning (fn. 3). Role binding is itself a performative naming act: the first call assigns the role, later calls must match or be rejected. L04 supplies neutral, court-adjacent vocabulary for the shared move: both Lexon contracts and VRCs choose the internal model, in which the artefact constitutes the obligation rather than describing it. L05's anti-comprehension thesis relocates understanding away from the machine and onto human readers, the same exclusion of the machine from semantics that the RPP performs.

**Divergences and the false friend.** The convergence stops at the mechanism, which forces the verdict. Lexon felicity is unilateral and substrate-borne: the Payer instantiates and appoints Payee and Arbiter in one constructor call, with no counterparty co-signature, no cost signal, no proverb, no personhood grounding; in trustful JavaScript mode the performative force collapses entirely and only the signed log survives as evidence (L01). RPP felicity is bilateral and counterparty-borne: two First Persons uttering mutual promises. L01 marks as conjecture that a Lexon instantiation is at best a degenerate one-sided ceremony. L04 never invokes Austin: his fidelity question is descriptive (does code match intent), not performative, and that absence is itself a finding. L05 dissolves the performative moment altogether: under Allen's contract stack, agreement is a case-by-case judicial reconstruction over the whole heap of utterances, with no privileged binding act, a genuine three-way disagreement among Lexon, the RPP, and Idelberger. So one discrete constitutive utterance-event is a claim Lexon and the RPP share against the surveyed literature, even while they disagree about its felicity condition. L05 also inverts the cardinality of understanding: he maximizes the audience (public legibility for counsel and laypersons) where the ceremony minimizes it (exactly two provers). Same relocation, opposite design goal.

**Mechanisms.** Four port concretely. The felicity split: distinguish execution felicity (substrate unstoppability) from relational felicity (bilateral assessment) as two registers a VRC can carry, with the micro-blockchain log supplying the execution half (L01). First-use role binding as the name-binding model in ceremony records (L01). L04's four-stage fidelity-failure taxonomy places readability at stage 2 (translation) and bilaterality at stage 1 and the pre-textual layer, supporting the conjecture, still a conjecture, that the two mechanisms compose rather than compete; bilateral co-derivation is a candidate for the residual validation step Clack says even the single-artefact strategy leaves open. L05's fifth criterion: add demonstrated understanding at formation to his four evaluation criteria; none of his three implementations can satisfy it, a Lexon text signed inside an agreement ceremony can.

**Surviving open questions.** Whether a Lexon instantiation is a degenerate one-sided ceremony, and whether the Multi-Signature account feature already permits a bilateral constructor without grammar changes (L01 Q1). Whether the ceremony precedes Clack's step of establishing ante hoc meaning or replaces it (L04 Q1). Where felicity conditions sit in a taxonomy with no speech acts (L04 Q5). Whether proverb derivation can be phrased in Surden's vocabulary as an automated prima facie assessment of party understanding, making the ceremony legible to this literature (L05 Q2).

## A2. Gates as promises: structural convergence

**Convergences.** The gate definition (L01, EXTENSIBILITY section) is Promise Theory in different vocabulary: a voluntary, per-account restriction that only the autonomous account owner can accept for itself, which the letter's fn. 75 calls the very nature of contracts. The lifecycle maps to polarity: gate_set and gate_prepare as the owner's (+) promise about its own behavior, gate_activate by the optional keeper as the (-) acceptance, gate_close under pre-declared rules as revocation. Activation with a keeper requires the consent of both owner and keeper, the one genuinely bilateral moment in the 2023 system. The Gate Interface exposes 24 on* event hooks (Table 4), a complete named enforcement surface for boundary promises, and gates achieve promise semantics without escrowed hostage value, eliminating the pay-in lock-up of the smart-contract pattern. L03 confirms the semantics from an author who never mentions Promise Theory: may denotes the single actor allowed to initiate a clause, enforcement is incentive rather than compulsion, and the filing office's reminder duty is optional and paid, promise-not-imposition arriving unprompted in a statutory instrument.

**Divergences and the false friend.** The word Promise is the corpus's cleanest false friend. Lexon's Promise is the weakest primitive in its ladder, below Commitment: uncovered, unblocked, non-expiring, forgivable only by the receiver. The VRC promise bundle is the strongest binding in agentprivacy: bilateral, co-signed, mutually assessed. Same word, opposite position in the strength ordering. Lexon promises and commitments are strictly unilateral, the receiver's only verbs being forgive, release, and deposit. The keeper is a third-party power channel able to update parameters and close the gate, so a gated obligation is co-administered, unlike a Swordsman boundary promise held by the sovereign alone. And gates constrain token use only: the convergence is exact at the bookkeeping layer and analogical above it.

**Mechanisms.** The graded strength ladder (Approval, Commitment, Promise) as three canonical strength classes for VRC promise typing. The gate lifecycle as boundary-promise lifecycle, with keeper activation as the minimal co-signature already native to Lexon and the natural splice point for full VRC co-signing. The hook-to-polarity map: onSending and onReceiving as (-) assessment points, onMaking and onCommitting as (+) emissions, onForgiving and onReleasing as dissolution, onReversing and onAcceding as accountability; this feeds HENNING_BRIEF probe (ii) directly. forgive(promissor) as a receiver-side unilateral release primitive for VRC release ceremonies.

**Surviving open questions.** The keeper's promise polarity: a (-) acceptance-promise to administer, or a standing imposition the owner pre-accepted; the answer decides whether a gated account is autonomous in the Bergstra/Burgess sense and whether gate re-use across unlimited accounts makes gates the VRC-template mechanism (L01 Q2). Whether one-initiator may semantics can express a co-signed bilateral bind at formation, or only the sequential offer-then-authorize pattern (L03 Q4, shared with A7).

## A3. Topology of trust and promise graphs: structural convergence

**Convergences.** L01 makes the topology argument empirically: complex smart contracts show star shapes (Figure 5, grounded in the author's Ethereum transaction browser at IBM, fn. 80), and per-account gates replace the star with a peer-to-peer web that grows incrementally (Figure 6). This is the bilateral-VRC argument against issuer-centric credentials made from the token side. Two properties deepen the promise-graph reading: authority accrues to exactly the parties who would lose from a change (edge governance by edge stakeholders), and gates compose while remaining independent and mutually oblivious, with the fn. 81 containment rule preventing a deeper gate from vetoing a transfer for everyone involved. Cross-contract promise density is native: one account simultaneously carries approvals, one commitment, multiple promises, a gate, and reversibility grants, so the same account is a node in many agreements at once, the promise-graph-spanning-contract-boundaries claim of the run's thesis instantiated.

**Divergences.** The web has a hidden hub: every gate, promise, and commitment lives inside one deployed token contract, so the star is removed at the relationship layer and reintroduced one stratum down. The honest reading of Figure 6 is topology of authority, not topology of data. Lexon's edges are also transactional and ad hoc, materializing per transfer, where the VRC graph is a persistent relationship graph. L05 confirms the persistence gap at grammar level: CONTRACTS per Licensee gives per-edge contracting, but memory is per-contract, not per-relationship, and two contracts with the same counterparty share nothing; the promise-graph reading across documents is therefore the run's extension, not any letter's claim.

**Mechanisms.** Stratum as promise popcount: define an account's stratum as the count of active promise-class objects on it, with Table 3 giving an enumerable basis; a CTR-LEX seed. The cascade containment rule as a portable boundedness condition on promise-graph cascade terms in V6, plus the design test that only edge endpoints may mutate an edge. The exponent conjecture, recorded as conjecture in L01: gate-webs raise the effective scaling exponent k in the stratum-weighted network term (1 + Σ wᵢnᵢ/N₀)^k relative to star topology, because each account contributes independent promise-making capacity, distributed O(N), where a star routes through one hub's fixed O(1) logic; falsification condition, simulate both topologies with equal promise counts and show equal value growth.

**Surviving open questions.** Whether gate-web topology changes the exponent k or only the multiplier inside the stratum sum, and whether the single shared token contract is a hidden hub the per-stratum weights already price in (L01 Q3).

## A4. Private evidence chains: structural convergence

**Convergences.** Three letters carry the same mechanism at three stages. L02 (Section 4.1.2) holds the origin statement: the business-logic layer must retain traces of deprecated previous truth, because prior records defend decisions made on the knowledge then available; its two-layer record, an immutable signed event list below and re-derivable business facts above, is the design ancestor. L01 specifies the shipped form: the micro blockchain, a hash-chained signed log held only by the parties, each entry carrying hash, timestamp, role, clause or noun, and signature, with the hash computed by SHA-256 over the entire log file up to that point, and the letter claiming contract state stays fully private at administrative cost magnitudes below a chain. State semantics is one line: relationship state is derivable from, and only from, the record chain (L01, fn. 56). L03 (Appendix C) shows a working implementation: per-actor RSA-SHA256 signatures, a rolling SHA-256 chain digest over the whole log, and bundle-and-send transport by email. Together these implement the reconstruction ceiling R < 1 as bookkeeping: full evidence for the parties, no state reconstruction for outsiders. Conjecture, marked in L01 and still standing: the micro blockchain realizes R < 1 structurally because the evidence chain never leaves the parties; falsifiable if a third party can reconstruct state from transport metadata alone.

**Divergences.** Gaps rather than paradigm differences, but real. Timestamps are self-declared plain text with no external anchor, and roles bind to any name, alias, number, or key, so the log proves key-continuity of a role and internal sequence, not personhood or wall-clock truth (L01). There is no consensus mechanism for near-simultaneous acts, and mutual copies mitigate log loss without supplying that consensus; the fix is co-signing discipline, not replication. The email transport leaks relationship metadata to mail providers: content privacy without graph privacy, with the planned peer-to-peer microclient as the intended repair. L03 adds a compile-target divergence on evidence retention: the Solidity Terminate_and_Clear uses selfdestruct, implementing clearance as contract destruction, the opposite of an append-only substrate, while the JavaScript target flags the instrument terminated and keeps the signed log.

**Mechanisms.** The 5-field log entry as ceremony-record serialization: role as participant, clause as promise event type in the contract's own vocabulary, signature as attestation, hash chain as integrity fraction; the candidate epsilon(pi) evidence substrate and a CTR-LEX seed. Bundle-and-send turn-passing as the minimal zero-infrastructure ceremony transport (L01). L02's two-layer record with retained previous truth as the shape of the substrate: an append-only information layer over which relationship state is deterministically re-derivable, giving defensible history.

**Surviving open questions.** Whether mutual copies plus cross-signing on every exchange can substitute for consensus on near-simultaneous entries, and whether the truncated 48-bit (12 hex character) hash prefix leaves a collision gap to flag for Diedrich (L01 Q4). Whether emailing the contract bundle defeats the privacy claim at the graph layer (same question). Who may read the full signed event list when a neutral is invoked, since arbitration-time disclosure could leak the relationship graph the log otherwise protects (L02 Q3).

## A5. Grammar to graph, the keystone: structural convergence, held at the conjectured register

**Convergences.** The losslessness leg of the keystone is multiply attested. L01 exhibits the micro-evidence directly: the Figure 10 escrow AST decomposes one subject into four typed predicates, four triples. L03 verifies the pass-through principle against an independent author: Lexon carries no representation of thought beyond the language input itself, the compiler processes the natural language as if it were the program, meaning rides in grammar and typed relations while nouns stay neutral markers; roles compile to key pairs, her type vocabulary (person to address, data to hash, time to timestamp, binary, amount, text) is a graph schema in miniature, and her Appendix A is a complete triple serialisation of a statutory instrument: readable form, executable code, and exact subgraph at once. L05 supplies the strongest third-party sentence: a critical scholar names direct AST mapping, with no intermediary layer, as the property preserving structure, syntax, and higher-order meaning, and his Prolog verdict states the run's thesis negatively: free-form facts without a controlled grammar collapse into arbitrary term-naming, and Lexon's TERMS section is exactly the missing system for setting facts, verbs, and rules. Two structural bonuses from L03: hash-as-reference implements commitment-without-disclosure inside a public record, a public-legible subgraph with opaque leaf content, the reconstruction ceiling drawn as topology and derived independently inside commercial law; and the puzzling-triviality effect, writing law as code forces explicit formalization of background relations humans never utter, so controlled grammar surfaces structure probabilistic extraction would systematically miss. L05's retargetability observation makes a triples or Cypher emitter simply a further normative target for an AST that already exists in the 0.2.12 compiler.

**Tempering (L04), which sets the register.** The keystone stands as conjectured, not proven. Clack records that Lexon aims at automation of performance rather than semantic analysis, that its formal syntax definition was still in development, and that its triples are operational edges only: the deontic layer, obligations over actions, is exactly what no action-oriented CNL/DSL has yet demonstrated, and exactly where the promise-graph overlay would sit; Clack independently identifies the gap the run proposes to fill. His three specification problems transfer directly: separability means graph edges need three aspect types (deontic, operational, temporal); isomorphism means clause-to-subgraph mapping is many-to-many; canonical form means semantically equal contracts may emit different subgraphs, so deduplication needs a canonicalisation pass. The Church-Rosser point limits the bare-triple reading to the declarative fraction of Lexon; order-bearing clause sequences need temporal edges, not bare triples. Conjecture, marked in L04.

**Divergences.** The meaning-location split is the axis's preserved divergence and the run's deepest. Lexon and the entire surveyed field keep meaning computable inside the four corners of the document; the RPP vests decisive meaning in the bilateral decoder outside any document. L04 shows the field has no slot for this: relationship-indexed meaning fits neither ambiguity nor vagueness in his taxonomy, precise inside the dyad and indeterminate outside it, a missing dimension that is R < 1 stated linguistically; his proposal to formalise implicature into the artefact is the exact opposite move. L05 sits at the far public-legible pole: an opaque proverb clause would score as a legibility defect under his criteria, where the RPP scores it as the point. The field-level gap is double (L05): no surveyed system supplies a formation layer, and none persists the party-pair relationship across contracts. The VRC slot is empty across the surveyed field.

**Mechanisms.** The definitions-block as node emitter and clauses as typed actor-may-verb-object edges (L03), with Appendices A and D as ready-made test fixtures. Closed enumerations, exhibited by the 15-value collateral category list, as first-class controlled vocabulary for node-label taxonomies. The canonicalisation pass as a hard requirement for the Lexon-to-graph compiler target. Three-aspect edge typing with promise and commitment edges as deontic meta-edges over operational action edges (L04). The extended meaning triad for V6 prose: ambiguous, vague, relationship-indexed. TERMS-as-ontology-declaration answering L05's Prolog complaint, with Listing 3 as the worked example. L04's 10-criterion language rubric, retained for scoring Lexon against a hypothetical RPP/MyTerms DSL side by side.

**Surviving open questions.** Whether the Lexon 0.3 grammar admits a canonical form, testable against the golden fixtures (L04 Q3). Whether Lexon 0.2.12 round-trips Listing 3 to triples with zero loss, and whether the bracket types carry enough discipline to serve as node classes without a mapping table (L05 Q3). Whether the deontic meta-level can ride as overlay edges with zero grammar changes, or the Promise and Commitment primitives already constitute a partial deontic layer inside the language (L04 Q4). Whether the claimed German and English grammars would emit identical triples, which the keystone needs to survive translation (L02 Q4). And the deepest, from L03 Q5, conjecture: increasing formalization forces implicit context explicit until what remains is exactly the bilateral proverb, a stable opaque remainder that is the floor keeping R below 1.

## A6. Accountable reversibility: structural convergence

**Convergences.** L02 specifies the pattern the brief predicted, present verbatim in structure: name the neutral (an ordinary published public key, reusable across unlimited contracts, multi-sig capable, analogized to a choice-of-law and choice-of-venue clause), bound the window (30 days in the example, settable to zero, gliding, transactions older than the window final), keep the default final (implicit escrow of payouts until the window passes). Four refinements converge with agentprivacy designs: the zero-preparation neutral, passive until invoked, matching Watcher Network designated observation; reorg semantics without schism, deterministic replay of business facts over an intact information layer, explicitly contrasted with the DAO hardfork; trace retention, the A4 origin; and finality as a consumer-protection variable, irreversibility protecting merchants and criminals rather than consumers, with the DAO's 27-day freeze as the empirical anchor that a bounded window is what makes recovery possible. The one quote this synthesis spends, because it is the cleanest bridge between forum keys and accountable privacy: "Reversibility is functionally a temporary change in the burden of proof" (L02, Section 4.1.9, p. 16). Discretion shifts who must prove what; the information layer is never edited. L01 shows the 2023 successor, the forum key, implemented at the token layer with owner-granted, time-windowed discretion for a designated forum such as a court or notary. L05 closes the loop from outside: even his minimal one-license test contract adds an ARBITER because vague provisions cannot be assessed automatically, the definition admits a natural person, a legal person, or a machine, and the Arbiter holds the only two power clauses; a designated third seat with bounded discretion appears to be a fixed point of realistic computable contracts.

**Divergences.** The brief's predicted divergence is confirmed by L02. First, trust locus: 2017 places discretion in a key-holding human institution chosen by the parties and celebrates the human, up to judges writing code; agentprivacy places it in protocol-level association proofs. Second, scope: arbitration by code lets the 2017 arbitrator retroactively amend contract code and rewrite the business-facts event list, bounded rewriting of business history, where Accountable Wallet and Watcher discretion never touch state; the 2017 power is closer to a time-boxed backdoor with an audit trail than to a disclosure valve. Third, internal to L02: the fractional-reserve extension deliberately lets clawbacks ripple into third-party accounts, in tension with the paper's own no-uncontrolled-ripple requirement. Two false friends inside Lexon's own vocabulary: escrow (automatic system-layer suspension, not an escrow agent) and reversibility itself (re-derivation of business facts over an immutable log, never chain rollback). Conjecture, marked in L02 and pending the full side-by-side: the 2023 forum key dropped event-list rewriting, arbitration-by-code, and the fractional reserve, shipping only the narrower reversible transfer by designated neutral.

**Mechanisms.** Gliding-window automatic escrow as a bounded contest window on VRC ceremony effects, finality the default once the window passes; the forum-key versus association-set CTR-LEX candidate. Designation by key inclusion: neutral selection as a unilateral choice-of-venue act with no onboarding, portable to Watcher and Accountable Wallet neutral selection. The burden-of-proof framing as V6 vocabulary for accountable privacy. The hybrid worth exploring: a neutral defined as a set-membership proof over published court keys, association-set trustlessness with forum-key accountability (L02 Q3).

**Surviving open questions.** Exactly which 2017 powers the 2023 forum key dropped, needed before the Diedrich session (L02 Q1). Whether the arbitration window can be expressed as a Promise Theory promise, both parties voluntarily promising a bounded discretion to the named neutral, making the system-layer escrow a kept promise rather than an imposition (L02 Q2).

## A7. Terms as executable law: structural convergence

**Convergences.** The machinery converges from two sides. L02 supplies the negotiation half: joint composition, parties exchanging machine-checkable drafts while an automated verifier answers how outcomes change under proposed edits, makes negotiation computable counter-offers instead of take-it-or-leave-it consent theatre; and semantic meaningfulness checking means an unintelligible term fails verification rather than being deemed accepted, the operational replacement for fictional assent. Its Section 3.2.1 states the consent-theatre diagnosis in Diedrich's own 2017 cypherpunk terms, metadata profiling against informational self-determination, consent given without protest: an independent derivation of the contractual privacy paradigm's premise and a strong plural-recognition anchor. L03 supplies the execution half: in her lapse, continuation, and clearing clauses the Lexon code simultaneously restates the statute and performs it, law-as-program literal, leaving no gap between the stated term and the executed term and hence no room for consent theatre. Her triage is the critical datum: 9 sections implemented, 6 reduced in risk, 6 left untouched; the executable/prose/adjudicative boundary MyTerms must walk, already walked once for secured transactions. Supporting convergences: jurisdiction-as-framework and the definable grammar (L02), the Academy-style governed vocabulary occupying the same governance slot as the MyTerms registry role, L03's transparency constraint meeting the readable-by-judges requirement at the artifact level, her point that executable need not mean contractual (statutory obligations perform without contract-formation doctrine), and L05's legal-looks gate: of his surveyed field only Lexon still reads as a contract to counsel, with Solidity and Prolog as the negative controls, so a MyTerms term-set serialized in anything else predictably fails with lawyers.

**Divergences.** Direction of authorship is the preserved divergence. Lexon 2017's authors are lawyers, lawmakers, judges, and eventually governmental academies, with the individual appearing only as beneficiary; Reyes executes public statute run by a public office whose continued relevance she deliberately retains for political feasibility. MyTerms starts from the person proffering. The convergence is in the machinery, not the author, and the Academy model is in tension with the paper's own cypherpunk grounding. Second, adjusting to jurisdiction is not surviving jurisdiction: jurisdiction-as-framework makes one contract behave differently per jurisdiction, while MyTerms wants the person's terms invariant with enforcement varying, and Civil Law clause-striking (L02 fn. 17) means framework injection can override proffered terms. Third, the missing anchor: Reyes's argument leans on a statutorily accepted safe-harbor form with no MyTerms analogue; the argument structure transfers, the anchor does not. Two warnings ride along: smart contract as a legal category is a false friend, since UETA accommodations effectively carved smart contracts out of coverage, cautioning against labeling VRC promise bundles contracts (L03); and legal-looks without demonstrated understanding can reinforce consent theatre, counsel honouring what looks legal while parties still never read it (L05 Q1).

**Mechanisms.** The Reyes triage taxonomy (implements, reduces risk of, does not interfere with) as the method for splitting a MyTerms term sheet into executable, prose, and adjudicative registers, applied per IEEE P7012 term before drafting its Lexon clause. The four-step compatibility ladder (mirror the accepted form, execute rules with definitions unchanged, name the single amendment, offer dual adoption routes) as the skeleton for a court-readable MyTerms agreement. The MyTerms-as-Lexon draft: an article list, Payer/Payee style, with the Swordsman as a named role, the choice-of-venue slot filled by key inclusion, and L03's three-clause sketch (marked as design-register conjecture, drafted by the chronicle, not present in the letter) plus L05's Listing 3 as syntactic templates; L05's machine-admitting ARBITER gives the Swordsman the Examine-for-Breach seat with Declare Breach reserved for humans. The PIP List plus key-signature release as the minimal upgrade path from unilateral effectiveness to conditionally bilateral effectiveness. The reminder-fee escrow, 9 lines of Lexon, as the template for priced, optional, refundable service promises. Triple rendering (one source, three synchronized renderings: controlled text, prose, executable) as the presentation pattern for a MyTerms whitepaper facing engineers and policy people at once.

**Surviving open questions.** What plays the safe-harbor role for MyTerms: can IEEE P7012 conformance function as the accepted form a court must not refuse, or is legislation unavoidable (L03 Q2). Whether one term-set can remain semantically invariant across divergent jurisdiction frameworks, who may author person-side frameworks with standing equal to state actors, and whether the framework direction can be inverted so the person's proffered terms are the framework a counterparty's contract must instantiate (L02 Q5). Where the executable/adjudicative line falls for a privacy agreement, breach declaration executable with remedy and forgiveness left as prose, and who the designated neutral is in a MyTerms deployment (L03 Q3). Whether may semantics can express a co-signed bilateral bind at formation (L03 Q4).

## A8. Anchoring below the agent: structural convergence

**Convergences.** L01 states the principle twice: interventions like reversal or partial diversion must anchor at the token-implementation level, and what an underlying system does not provide, a language cannot support, with the database-locking analogy (application-level integrity is impossible without atomic storage primitives) as supporting argument. This is identical in form to the three-axis separation claim: guarantees anchor beneath the layer they constrain. L02 holds the origin: reversibility as a separate layer between blockchain and application, with irreversibility retained as the enabling foundation, on the TCP-over-IP analogy. L01's trustless membrane names the boundary concept: the region of functionality performable in one coherently protected sequence without trusting a third party, with the token's job being to extend its scope. On determinism, Lexon self-positions as the symbolic complement to generative AI, with ML relegated to the preparatory writing phase: structurally the Swordsman/Mage split with Lexon on the Swordsman side.

**Divergences.** Who writes the boundary is the preserved divergence, and L01 is unambiguous about its side: the Robotic Laws are state-authored, universal, and democratically approved, laws written in Lexon by elected lawmakers and embedded verbatim in autonomous machines; the Swordsman charter is sovereign-authored and per-person. Same mechanism, aimed by different hands: Lexon anchors public law below private agents, agentprivacy anchors private boundaries below public exposure. This is the corpus's clearest genuine disagreement and should be preserved, not resolved. Flags riding with it: the trustless membrane is execution-scoped atomicity, mapping most cleanly onto the agent axis alone (conjecture, marked in L01), where the three-axis separation is a standing multiplicative property; unbiased in L01 is a determinism claim, not a values claim, since bias moves upstream into whoever writes the Lexon text; L02's AI-judges ambition diverges from the deterministic/probabilistic separation, a tension internal to that letter, which itself concedes the fallibility of trained AI. L05 adds a vocabulary guard: Allen's contract stack, an interpretive evidence heap for courts, is a false friend for the agentprivacy layer stack, an architecture of guarantees anchored beneath the layer they constrain; they share the word stack and little else. L04 adds the layer warning: even a perfect top-of-stack grammar-to-graph mapping says nothing about fidelity below it, since compilers, optimisers, and runtimes can each betray the layer above.

**Mechanisms.** The anchoring evidence line, obligations enforced at layer n require primitives at layer n-1, as the L01 citation for the V6 anchoring claim-cluster, with its falsification condition: exhibit a contract-layer-only mechanism achieving reversal or diversion with equal guarantees. Corollaries: Swordsman boundary promises should compile to substrate-level hooks rather than agent-level policy; the trustless membrane becomes a nameable, measurable V5 perimeter concept; and stacked gates permit the coexistence test, a state-authored Robotic-Law gate and a sovereign charter gate on one account, a concrete joint-work probe for the Diedrich session.

**Surviving open questions.** Whether the claim that obligations require account-level anchoring is provable or an efficiency claim; V6 discipline requires classing it before citing, and the same test applies to whether Lexon's determinism survives oracle-fed gate triggers (L01 Q5).
