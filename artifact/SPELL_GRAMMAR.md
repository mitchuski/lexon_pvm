## Conventions (SG-1..SG-6, folded from run lexr3, promise-bundle-spell lever, 2026-07-11)

Keystone fold of the lever's proposedSpellGrammar (VALIDATED 8/8, critic
class structural; every worked instance GATE PASS and RELATION PASS under
the mutation probe). The proposer's text, keystone-reviewed:

A spell is a Lexon contract whose clauses are exactly one promise bundle. The stub's design intuition (a seat card or skill trigger IS a promise bundle) holds under the base gate and under the mutation probe; six conventions carry it:

SG-1 Modal mapping. 'may' = offered capability (voluntary permission, promise polarity +give); 'must' = binding duty (commitment); a recital action = a commitment performed at instantiation. A seat card's Writes list renders as may-clauses; its Definition of done renders as must-clauses.

SG-2 The recital is the shared root (T3 analog). Everything all parties share (the appointing principal, the one shared object such as the Terms, the Shared Poem, the Shared Content) is placed before the first CLAUSE; nothing else is shared below it.

SG-3 Trust gates cross the separation. A gated capability is written ', if <Binary> is declared:' and the Binary is declared in a SEPARATE clause by the assessing side, never by the gated role (no self-approval). Claimed as {type:gate}. Harness reading: the Mage's Project clause gates on the Swordsman's Boundary Approved declaration, never the reverse. Two-stage gates chain: the assessor's declaring clause may itself be gated on the subject's filed evidence (Trust Tier: Advance gates on Signal Record filed; Exercise gates on Tier Advanced).

SG-4 Ceremonies are single-officiant ordered chains. One sentence, one actor, actions joined ', and afterwards' in the source's canonical phase order; claimed as {type:ordering}. Multi-actor rites do NOT go in one ordering claim (triples interleave per actor); they split into one ungated clause per side plus one gated closing clause under SG-3 (Moon Ceremony: Trace Apart, Trace Again, Decide, Draw).

SG-5 Stakes are escrow-shaped and irreversibility is an absence. Commitment = 'pay the Stake into escrow' (gated on qualification); slash = 'pay from escrow the Stake to' the collective side; the role's inability to reclaim is carried by NO clause routing the stake back, claimed as {type:absence, to:Role, what:Stake}. A seat card's Hard rules (what the seat can never do) render as absence claims, the same shape as the folded T-148 direction rule.

SG-6 Bilateral formation is a defined-predicate conjunction. A relationship-status term (VRC-shaped) binds '"Relationship" is this contract.', defines the status over >= 2 independently produced facts joined by 'and' (each fact filed or declared in its own clause by its own side), and gates issuance on 'this Relationship is <Status>' with 'The A or the B may' as issuer when no central authority exists. Claimed as {type:conjunction}.

Worked instances (this run, all GATE PASS + RELATION PASS): Invitation (SG-1..3, gate), Trust Tier (SG-3 two-stage, gate), Moon Ceremony (SG-3+SG-4 split form, gate), Evoke Ceremony (SG-4, ordering), Guardian Charter (SG-5, absence), Verifiable Relationship Credential (SG-6, conjunction).
