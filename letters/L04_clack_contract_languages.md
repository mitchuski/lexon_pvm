# L04: Languages for Smart and Computable Contracts (Clack, 2021)

**Status:** fetch. **URL:** https://arxiv.org/ftp/arxiv/papers/2104/2104.03764.pdf
**Axes:** A1, A5. **Output:** `out/L04_convergence.md`

Clack is the neutral cartographer: a UCL survey of the language design space, not an advocacy piece. Use it to position both Lexon and the RPP on the same map, and to stress-test the semantic-gap problem that both projects claim to solve.

## Read for

1. **The fidelity question (A1).** Clack's core question: how can there be confidence that code is faithful to the intentions of the parties? Lexon answers with readability (the text is the code). The agreement ceremony answers with bilaterality (both parties sign what both parties derived). Extract Clack's taxonomy of where fidelity fails (drafting, translation, execution, interpretation) and place each answer on it. Conjecture to test: readability and bilaterality address different failure stages and compose rather than compete.
2. **Semantics of natural versus computer languages (A5).** Clack explores the semantic gap between legal and computational disciplines. This is the keystone axis territory. Map three positions: (a) Clack's spectrum of DSLs and controlled languages, (b) Lexon's meaning-in-use structuralism (nouns as markers), (c) the RPP's meaning-in-relationship (proverbs as compressed shared context). Position (c) is more radical than (b): Lexon removes meaning from nouns but keeps it computable; the RPP removes meaning from the credential entirely and vests it in the bilateral decoder. Record whether Clack's framework has a slot for (c) or whether it falls outside his taxonomy. Either answer is a finding.
3. **Computable Contracts methodology.** Clack distinguishes smart contracts (automation) from computable contracts (a specific methodology). Determine where MyTerms agreements sit: they are computable (machine-readable, deterministic matching) before they are smart (no chain required). This supports the contractual privacy paradigm framing: MyTerms is computable contracting for the personal data relationship.

## Watch for

- Clack's criteria for evaluating contract languages. Reuse them as an evaluation rubric in the synthesis: score Lexon and a hypothetical RPP/MyTerms DSL against the same criteria.
